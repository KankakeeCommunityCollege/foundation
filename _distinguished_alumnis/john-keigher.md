Following a career defined by executive leadership, entrepreneurial success, and unwavering commitment to servant leadership, John Keigher has been named the 2026 John M. Fulton Distinguished Alumni Award recipient by the Kankakee Community College Foundation.

A graduate of Kankakee Community College with an Associate in Applied Science degree in Computer Information Systems, Keigher built an accomplished career spanning more than three decades across transportation, manufacturing, financial services, and consumer products. Widely respected for his strategic vision and results-driven leadership, he has helped organizations scale operations, accelerate growth, and create long-term enterprise value.

Keigher served as director and partner of Midwest Transit Equipment, Inc., helping build the company into a globally recognized transportation industry leader. Then, as CEO of Urban Farmer, Keigher guided the company through substantial growth and helped establish it as a nationally recognized leader in “better-for-you” food manufacturing. Following a distinguished executive career, Keigher remains active as a fractional executive, strategic advisor, and consultant to emerging and growth-stage companies.

“Across every aspect of his career and service, John exemplifies the values of leadership, innovation, integrity, and lifelong commitment to education,” said Nancy Schunke, director of alumni engagement at KCC. “His impact on Kankakee County and the surrounding region has been significant and enduring, making him a truly deserving recipient of this honor.”

Keigher has also continually shown a deep commitment to community and workforce development. He currently serves in numerous leadership roles, including:

*	Village trustee and finance chairman, Village of Roanoke, Illinois 
*	Board member and governance committee chair, KCC Foundation Board
*	Board member, Midland Institute for Entrepreneurship
*	Board chair, Kankakee County CEO Program; he helped establish in Kankakee County
*	Founding board member, Heart of Illinois CEO
*	Board member, Roanoke-Benson Hope Center

Previously, Keigher served as president of the Kankakee County Chamber of Commerce and was a longtime board member of United Way of Kankakee and Iroquois Counties. He served in many other civic and nonprofit leadership roles.

A passionate advocate for entrepreneurship and workforce readiness, Keigher has played a pivotal role in mentoring and developing future business leaders through the CEO Program, helping connect students with real-world business experiences, mentorship, and entrepreneurial opportunities.

“John’s impact extends far beyond his professional achievements,” said Dr. Kay Green, Chair of the KCC Foundation Board. “He leads with humility, invests deeply in others, and consistently uses his talents and resources to strengthen our community. His service to the Foundation and commitment to student success reflect the very best of what this award represents.”

John and his wife, Shanon, are proud supporters and donors to the KCC Foundation.

“John Keigher represents the very best of Kankakee Community College,” said Dr. Michael Boyd, President of Kankakee Community College. “From his beginnings as a student in our Computer Information Systems program to his success as a CEO, entrepreneur, and respected community leader, John has remained committed to lifting others and creating opportunity. His leadership has left a lasting mark on this region.”
