KCC Staff Alumni<br>
Senior Administrative Specialist, Business Affairs<br>
’23 KCC Graduate

**How did you decide KCC was right for you?**

I knew I wanted to obtain my first college degree, and with KCC being local and affordable, it was a no-brainer. Being able to transfer credits from a previous institution also made it possible for me to cut down on my course load.

**Why was KCC a good choice for you?**

KCC was more than a school for me; it was where I began to grow mindful of the world. The campus environment and student life gave me a healthy balance while keeping me focused on my goals. I joined organizations that matched my interests and still stayed committed to my coursework. The professors and staff genuinely cared about their work and their relationships with students, and I always felt supported, whether by an advisor, a professor, a staff member, or even a friendly face on the way to class.

**What advice would you give a student considering KCC?**

Don't even think twice about it - sign up today. It's hard to find a college where not only is it affordable, but the quality of education is also exceptional. It's the best decision a person could make!

**What is your role in your position?**

As a student, I worked in the Hammes Bookstore in the summer of 2022. I also worked at the Miner Memorial Library in the spring and summer of 2023. Once graduated, I came back to KCC in the fall of 2024 as a staff member of the Continuing Education and Business Partnerships office, and currently I am the Senior Administrative Specialist for Business Affairs.

**What is the most rewarding part of your job?**

I love knowing that every little function of my job ultimately impacts the students we serve at KCC, making this a better place for everyone. Even if I am not in a student-facing role, I know that every duty is for the benefit of our students and the entire institution.

**During your time working at KCC, what are some of the events or moments that are most memorable?**

One of the most meaningful parts of my role is watching students interact with patients during clinicals and seeing their confidence grow. Giving students their completion letters at the end of the semester is a proud moment, but what stays with me most is when a student sincerely says, “Thank you.” I always respond, “You did this—I was here to guide you,” because their growth and success belong to them.
